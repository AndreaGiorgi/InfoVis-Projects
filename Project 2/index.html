Radiation levels in a week intervall
obtained from norwegian government data

Radiation level monitoring became of major importance after the Chernobyl events of 1986 and more so after the Fukushima events of 2011. The Norwegian government, as well as the Swedish, Danish and Finnish governments, monitors radiation levels throughout the country on a daily basis throughout various monitoring stations. Attention to this issue has increased starting from 22/23 June 2020, when monitoring stations detected abnormal levels of cesium-134, cesium-137, cobalt-60 and ruthenium-103 (source) in the North and Norwegian sea.
